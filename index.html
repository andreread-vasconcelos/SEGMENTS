Prologue

SEGMENTS is conceived as an archive of investigations—an evolving body of work exploring infrastructure, landscape, mobility, and the philosophical dimensions of the built environment. Each work is a fragment of a larger inquiry into how architecture can respond to the realities of the present while revealing those yet to come.

The built environment records conflict, scarcity, ambition, and hope. War, poverty, and economic systems become spatial before they become visible. Waste space and wasteland are not voids, but territories whose potential has not yet been articulated.

Geometry offers order; amusement and discovery preserve uncertainty. Transport is physical, mental, and civic. Every threshold is both portal and proposition—a gate between what exists and what might.
