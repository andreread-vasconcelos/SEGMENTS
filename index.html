Prologue

SEGMENTS is conceived as an archive of investigations—an evolving body of work exploring infrastructure, landscape, mobility, and the philosophical dimensions of the built environment. Each work is a fragment of a larger inquiry into how architecture can respond to the realities of the present while revealing those yet to come.

War, poverty, and economic systems become spatial before they become visible.

Waste space and wasteland are not voids, but territories whose potential has not yet been articulated.

Geometry offers order; amusement and discovery preserve uncertainty.

Means of transport become mental transport, physical transport, and civic imagination.

Every threshold is both a portal and a proposition.

About the author

SEGMENTS ARCHITECT

Andre is very contemplative. In solitude, he analyses the world and his surroundings with great care. Observation has always preceded creation; long before architecture became a discipline, it was simply a way of understanding the world.

At the age of seven, he received a drawing book as a belated birthday gift. Like many children, he had hoped for toys instead. A book seemed like a disappointment. Reluctantly, he opened it.

One page changed everything.

It revealed the complete anatomy of a horse—its skeleton and musculature meticulously illustrated, as though its skin had vanished. That image filled him with an overwhelming sense of wonder. It was the first time a drawing revealed that imagination could coexist with knowledge, and that beauty could emerge from understanding.

On the back of the book, his godfather had written two words: INSPIRATION × TRANSPIRATION. At seven years old, they meant very little.

Within a year, he had painstakingly reproduced nearly every drawing the book contained. Among them, one remained unforgettable: the face of a madman. Decades later, he can still picture it with remarkable clarity.

As time passed, he continued to develop his ability to draw, but he devoted even more energy to cultivating his imagination. Drawing became a tool; imagination became the destination.

SEGMENTS is the result of that journey—a body of work shaped by discipline, curiosity, and the persistent desire to imagine worlds that do not yet exist.

If these investigations leave the visitor with a renewed sense of curiosity, then they have fulfilled their purpose.
