Manifesto

SEGMENTS is conceived as an archive of investigations—an evolving body of work exploring infrastructure, landscape, mobility, and the philosophical dimensions of the built environment.

War, poverty, and economic systems become spatial before they become visible.

Waste space and wasteland are territories whose potential has not yet been articulated.

Geometry offers order; amusement and discovery preserve uncertainty.

Means of transport become mental transport, physical transport, and civic imagination.

Every threshold is both a portal and a proposition.

Leisure

SEGMENTS ARCHITECT

Andre is contemplative. In solitude, he studies the world and his surroundings with care. Observation has always preceded creation; long before architecture became a discipline, it was a way of understanding the world.

At seven, he received a drawing book as a belated birthday gift. He had hoped for toys. Reluctantly, he opened it.

One page changed everything.

It revealed the anatomy of a horse—its skeleton and musculature meticulously illustrated, as though its skin had vanished. The drawing revealed that imagination could coexist with knowledge, and that beauty could emerge from understanding.

On the back, his godfather had written two words: INSPIRATION × TRANSPIRATION.

Within a year, he had reproduced nearly every drawing the book contained. Drawing became a tool; imagination became the destination.

SEGMENTS is the result of that journey—a body of work shaped by discipline, curiosity, and the persistent desire to imagine worlds that do not yet exist.
